Meal Preparation Tips

Here are some helpful tips for meal prepping:
  • It's much easier with a partner. Typically, one person chops and does small tasks while the other takes point on the full meals themselves. If you're not working with a partner, consider doing your chopping ahead of time.
  • Commit a couple of hours to this! Home cooked meals take effort, but they're well worth it. The idea behind meal prepping is that you take a solid chunk of time to prepare all your meals for the week.
  • Invest in some good tupperware, especially if you want to take your meals for lunch during the day.
  • Keep some easy-to-make food around for when you get tired of whatever meals you've made that week. Something like spaghetti or frozen pizza can be an easy change of pace mid-week that doesn't take a lot of time to prep.
